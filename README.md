# Lawbworld

> Where repos build cities.

**Follow us on X:** [@lawbworld](https://x.com/lawbworld)

**Lawbworld** is a 3D pixel-art voxel city where every building is a real open-source repository from the [gitlawb](https://gitlawb.com) network. The whole city — all **2566 repos** — is alive on screen, laid out by rarity, with downtown reserved for the most legendary projects and the outskirts for the long tail.

You don't just look at the city. You **claim it**, one building at a time.

---

## The NFT

Each building in Lawbworld is a unique ERC-721 NFT on **Base**.

- **One repo → one NFT.** Once a repo is minted, no one else can claim that exact building.
- **On-chain stats** are baked in at mint: rarity tier, file size, and a level (1–10) that you can grow over time.
- **Generated voxel art.** At mint, the server generates a one-of-a-kind 3D pixel-art voxel image of *your* building and pins it to the token's metadata. No two are alike.
- **Royalty-aware.** ERC-2981 royalty support is built in, so secondary sales on any marketplace flow back into Lawbworld.
- **Signed mints.** Rarity and size are signed off-chain by the Lawbworld server before they hit the contract, so nobody can forge a Legendary mint from a 1-KB repo.

### Rarity tiers

The city is shaped like a real one — a small downtown of icons, surrounded by rings of progressively more common neighborhoods.

| Tier | Share | Where it lives |
|---|---:|---|
| Legendary | 4 %  | Downtown core |
| Epic      | 20 % | Inner ring |
| Rare      | 40 % | Mid ring |
| Uncommon  | 24 % | Outer ring |
| Common    | 12 % | Suburbs |

### What an NFT actually does

Owning a Lawbworld building is not a flat collectible. It plugs into a live game economy:

- **Upgrade** your building level 1 → 10 by burning `$LAWBWORLD`. Higher levels mean a taller, denser voxel skyline and more leaderboard weight.
- **Leaderboard score** = rarity × level × stars × log(size). Holding rare repos and leveling them up climbs the global city ranking.
- **Arcade access** — owners get power-ups and entries in the *City Dash* mini-game and future arcade titles.
- **Gifting & trading** — transfer buildings peer-to-peer, or trade on any ERC-721 marketplace on Base.
- **Battle stats** — each building's repo stats (stars, forks, size, age) translate into HP / ATK / DEF / SPD for upcoming PvE and PvP battles.
- **Staking** — lock your building to earn `$LAWBWORLD` emissions, with APR scaling by rarity and level.

## $LAWBWORLD

`$LAWBWORLD` is the in-world currency, launched on the **Bankr** launchpad.

It's the only thing that makes the city move:

- **Burned to upgrade** buildings with `$LAWBWORLD`
- **Earned by staking** owned buildings.
- **Spent in the arcade** — entry fees, continues, cosmetic skins, leaderboard boosts.
- **Used in battles** for revives, items, and reward pools.
- **Gift currency** — send `$LAWBWORLD` alongside a building when transferring to a friend.

The supply sink is real: every upgrade *burns* tokens, so as the city grows, supply shrinks.

## Vision

Open source is the largest unowned commons on the planet. Every repo is a real building someone poured time into — and yet, off the cloners and the stars, almost nothing of that value ever flows back to the people who care about it.

**Lawbworld turns the open-source graph into a place.**

A place you can walk through. A place where projects you love are landmarks. A place where claiming a building, leveling it up, fighting for it, or gifting it to a friend means something — because the city is finite, the rarities are real, and the economy underneath it is on-chain.

Our north star:

1. **Make open-source legible.** Anyone — not just devs — should be able to explore the world of code as a world, not a list.
2. **Reward the long tail.** Not just the top 100 repos, but the 2,566+ that make the network actually work.
3. **One coherent economy.** Mint, upgrade, stake, battle, gift, arcade — all powered by a single token (`$LAWBWORLD`) with a real sink.
4. **Built on Base.** Cheap, fast, mainstream-ready. The city should feel like a game, not a blockchain demo.
5. **Composable.** Buildings are standard ERC-721. Anyone can build their own games, dashboards, or visualizations on top of the Lawbworld city.

The endgame is simple: when someone says "show me what open source looks like," we want the answer to be **Lawbworld**.
